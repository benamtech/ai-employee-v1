# Graphic Design And Social Media Intern Handoff

Status: archived reference

Audience: graphic design, social media, lightweight brand/content support.

Purpose: orient an intern quickly so they can make AMTECH visuals, social posts, simple decks, screenshots, and content ideas without drifting away from the product philosophy.

## AMTECH In One Sentence

AMTECH turns frontier AI agents into trusted AI employees for modern American small businesses.

The first customer wedge is owner-operated service businesses - painters, landscapers, contractors, and similar operators who lose time to estimates, follow-up, invoices, scheduling, customer messages, and admin work.

## Product Philosophy

AMTECH is not a chatbot, AI wrapper, or automation dashboard.

AMTECH should feel like hiring a capable office employee who:

- knows the business context;
- drafts and prepares work;
- asks for missing details;
- shows previews before sending, spending, or changing anything risky;
- leaves proof after work is done;
- works through text messages, web, signed links, and future surfaces.

The owner should think: **my employee is handling the office work, and I can inspect or approve anything important.**

## North Star

**The Macintosh of AI agents for the modern American business.**

This means:

- powerful but understandable;
- technical underneath, humane on the surface;
- precise, not playful;
- trusted, not magical;
- business-native, not developer-native;
- premium, but not luxury for luxury's sake.

## What We Are Selling

The first proof object is not "AI" in the abstract. It is concrete work:

- estimates and proposals;
- customer replies;
- follow-up reminders;
- invoices and payment links;
- simple bookkeeping/accounting help;
- connected-account repair and status;
- outputs, receipts, and proof.

The bigger product is one employee that can keep expanding into more office work as connectors, tools, and business context improve.

## Audience

Design for a busy owner-operator, not a tech buyer.

They may be:

- driving between jobs;
- answering customers from a phone;
- trying to get estimates out tonight;
- skeptical of AI hype;
- practical, time-starved, and allergic to software complexity.

Good AMTECH content respects that reality. It should communicate leverage, clarity, and trust.

## Brand Feel

Core aesthetic: **Bloomberg terminal meets early Stripe.**

Use:

- high contrast;
- sharp edges;
- strong typography;
- structured grids;
- hairline dividers;
- simple screenshots or product-like compositions;
- restrained red accents;
- confident, direct copy.

Avoid:

- purple AI gradients;
- glowing orbs;
- cute robots;
- soft pastel SaaS cards;
- bubbly rounded UI;
- generic "AI magic" language;
- stock-photo business people shaking hands;
- cluttered prompt screenshots with raw developer language.

## Hard Visual Rules

These are stricter for intern work than for product-engineering exploration.

- Use Inter for main text and AMTECH wordmarks.
- Use IBM Plex Mono only for small labels, timestamps, proof IDs, or operational captions.
- Use only these normal text/background modes:
  - black or near-black text on white;
  - white text on AMTECH red.
- AMTECH red: `#E11D2A`.
- Near-black: `#0a0a0a`.
- White: `#ffffff`.
- Light gray `#f4f4f4` is allowed for subtle section breaks.
- No gradients.
- No violet/purple.
- No rounded pill-heavy design.
- Keep layouts square, precise, and typographic.

## Copy Rules

Use owner language:

- employee;
- work;
- output;
- proof;
- estimate;
- invoice;
- customer;
- connected account;
- waiting for you;
- ready to send;
- needs approval.

Avoid developer/product-internal language:

- MCP;
- API;
- runtime;
- toolset;
- schema;
- token;
- config;
- stack trace;
- agent framework;
- raw JSON.

Avoid hype phrases:

- "10x your business overnight";
- "replace your team";
- "AI magic";
- "autopilot everything";
- "never work again."

Better copy pattern:

```text
Your AI employee drafts the estimate, shows you the preview, and waits for approval before it sends.
```

## Content Themes

Strong themes:

- "Text your office employee."
- "Approve before it sends."
- "Every output leaves proof."
- "The work surface for an AI employee."
- "For owners who do not have time to manage software."
- "Estimates, follow-up, invoices, and receipts from one employee."
- "AI that asks before it acts."

Good post types:

- simple before/after: messy inbox -> AMTECH work queue;
- estimate workflow: request -> draft -> approval -> sent -> proof;
- owner pain point: "It's 8:43pm and three estimates are still waiting";
- product principle: "Approval is a feature, not friction";
- screenshot-led posts with one strong caption;
- short founder notes about building AI employees for small businesses.

## Product Surface Cheat Sheet

- **Work Surface:** the owner desk. Shows what the employee is doing, what needs approval, outputs, connected accounts, tasks, proof, and activity.
- **Signed Review Link:** mobile page opened from SMS. Shows one specific draft/output/action with approve/reject/reply controls.
- **Outputs:** estimates, documents, invoices, messages, media, reports, and receipts.
- **Connected Accounts:** email, payments, accounting, files, calendar, and future systems the employee can work through.
- **Admin Console:** internal AMTECH operator tool. Do not use this in public marketing unless explicitly asked.

## Social Media Guardrails

- Do not promise fully autonomous action without approval.
- Do not imply AMTECH sends customer messages, spends money, changes records, or deletes data without gates.
- Do not show private customer data, phone numbers, addresses, emails, tokens, IDs, or raw logs.
- Do not make legal, financial, or tax guarantees.
- Do not position AMTECH as replacing humans. Position it as giving small-business owners leverage and a reliable office employee.
- If using screenshots, crop or redact anything sensitive and keep the composition clean.

## Practical Assignment Prompt

Use this when starting a design/content task:

```text
Create AMTECH graphic/social concepts for busy small-business owners.

AMTECH is the Macintosh of AI agents for the modern American business: a precise, trusted AI employee that drafts work, asks for approval before risky actions, and leaves proof.

Use a high-contrast black/white/red system, Inter typography, IBM Plex Mono for small operational labels, sharp layouts, no gradients, no purple, no cute AI imagery, and no developer jargon.

Communicate concrete work: estimates, customer replies, invoices, follow-up, connected accounts, approvals, outputs, and proof.

Tone: direct, confident, practical, premium, and owner-native.
```
