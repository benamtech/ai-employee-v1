# Free Infrastructure + Managed AI Employee Strategy

Status: canonical product and GTM strategy
Updated: 2026-07-17

## Category

AMTECH installs always-on intelligent software workers for owner-operated small businesses. It is not primarily an estimator, chatbot, CRM, automation builder, or bundle of AI tools.

The employee works inside the business's software and paperwork:

```text
notice work
-> interpret it with business memory and policy
-> prepare the next useful result
-> validate and organize proof
-> ask the owner at the trust, money, or reputation gate
-> take the approved action
-> remember what happened
```

Painting and landscaping contractors are the first beachhead because the back-office pain is concrete: estimates after dinner, unanswered leads, follow-up, email, invoices, deposits, schedules, and scattered proof.

## Canonical offer

### Start free

The free path gives one business a useful AI Employee with bounded usage. It is not a fake demo and should be capable of completing real, low-risk work inside clear limits.

The free path exists to let an owner experience the new category without buying an enterprise platform or committing to a long implementation. It should prove:

- the employee understands the business;
- the owner can text it a job;
- it prepares concrete work and proof;
- it asks before consequential actions;
- the relationship is useful enough to keep.

Exact quotas and abuse controls are product policy and must be enforced before being advertised as hard guarantees. Candidate controls include model usage, browser/computer use, messages, schedules, concurrency, connected-tool volume, and high-risk actions.

### Managed AI Employee — from $400/month

The managed employee is the flagship commercial path.

The owner is not paying for another seat or dashboard. The owner is paying for a maintained worker relationship:

- managed business context and connected tools;
- higher capacity and priority;
- scheduled and event-driven work;
- credential rotation and recovery;
- operational monitoring and repair;
- help adapting the employee to the business;
- commercial support and clearer service boundaries.

The starting price is **$400/month**. Higher capacity, difficult integrations, browser/computer procedures, multiple workflows, or higher support requirements may cost more. Pricing must remain plain-English and tied to delivered work, not an arbitrary feature matrix.

### Workforce — custom

Multiple employees, locations, roles, approval structures, or materially higher volume are sold as a managed workforce with custom scope and pricing.

There is no canonical $29–$99 assistant tier. AMTECH is using a useful free wedge and a serious managed-service floor rather than competing as a low-cost chatbot subscription.

## Why this is the flagship GTM path

### It removes the category-risk tax

Most owners do not know how to evaluate an “AI platform.” They can evaluate whether an employee drafted the estimate, noticed the customer reply, prepared the follow-up, and asked for approval. Starting free replaces abstract claims with first-hand proof.

### It matches how small businesses buy

An owner wants the office work handled, not a configuration project. The managed path packages connection, maintenance, recovery, and adaptation around the employee, which is where the durable value and trust live.

### It creates a natural conversion event

Conversion should happen when the employee becomes operationally important: the owner wants more capacity, more connected tools, scheduled/event-driven work, faster recovery, or help maintaining the system. The upgrade is from useful worker to managed worker, not from crippled demo to usable product.

### One additional job can justify the price

For service contractors, the economic story should be concrete: an evening returned, a lead followed up before a competitor, an estimate sent sooner, or one additional won job. Do not publish unsupported ROI guarantees; demonstrate the work and let the owner connect it to their own economics.

## Default capability envelope

The employee should be broadly useful wherever the work is computer- or document-mediated. Capability is constrained by scoped identity, policy, approval gates, budgets, controlled egress, and proof requirements rather than by removing every useful tool.

Potential capabilities include business memory, files, email, calendar, estimates, proposals, invoices, payments, bookkeeping evidence, reminders, browser/computer procedures, Manager tools, MCP tools, skills, scheduling, event handling, approvals, and work resources.

A capability is not marketed as live-accepted merely because its source exists. Provider/runtime claims require real current proof IDs and artifacts.

## Contractor work examples

The first website and sales demonstrations should center on work an owner recognizes immediately:

- turn a voice note, photos, and business rules into an estimate draft;
- notice an unanswered customer email and prepare the reply;
- prepare a follow-up when an estimate has gone quiet;
- organize job photos, assumptions, and customer history into one proof card;
- draft an invoice or deposit request and ask before it is sent;
- schedule a reminder or next action;
- surface what needs the owner's decision and keep routine work moving.

The estimate remains an effective first proof object. It is not the product category.

## Trust boundary

The governing promise is:

> It can prepare work. You approve the actions that touch customers, money, or reputation.

The product should make visible:

- what the employee noticed;
- what it prepared;
- which business facts it used;
- what requires approval;
- what action occurred;
- the resulting provider or system proof.

Provider credentials remain in AMTECH-controlled custody. Employee runtimes receive scoped credentials and policy, not master keys.

## Public estimator status

The public estimator is an outdated acquisition and regression experiment. It may remain useful for narrow public-ingress, artifact, provisioning, or runtime regressions, but it is not canonical for:

- product category;
- website information architecture;
- onboarding UX;
- employee profile design;
- pricing;
- normal-employee launch acceptance.

Do not route future GTM or website strategy around it.

## Website implication

The public website must sell the worker relationship through concrete work, visible proof, owner control, and economic relevance. It should lead with owner pain and the result, then explain the mechanism and security boundaries.

Canonical language:

- **Hero:** “Your business gets an employee that lives in the software.”
- **Support:** “Text it the job. It writes the estimate, follows up, organizes the proof, and asks before anything leaves your business.”
- **Primary CTA:** “Build my AI Employee”
- **Offer:** “Start free. Managed AI Employee from $400.”

## Current acceptance caveat

The free + $400 strategy is canonical. Enforcement, production reliability, and provider/runtime acceptance still require the current now-to-live work and fresh proof. Public copy may show clearly labeled product demonstrations and source-wired workflows, but must not imply live provider/customer acceptance where no current proof exists.
