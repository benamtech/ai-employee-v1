# Free Infrastructure + Managed Workforce Strategy

Status: canonical product and GTM strategy
Updated: 2026-07-16

## Category

AMTECH provides managed AI employee infrastructure. It is not primarily a painting estimator, CRM, chatbot, or workflow builder. Painting is the first vertical package; contractor-like businesses and bookkeeping are the initial market, not the platform boundary.

The product model is:

```text
event
-> interpret with business context and policy
-> choose a skill, connector, browser, or computer-use procedure
-> execute
-> validate
-> materialize proof and work
-> ask the owner only when consequential
-> continue
```

A generated employee can include an isolated Hermes runtime, continuous identity, business brain and filesystem, email, Google Workspace and Calendar, Stripe, QuickBooks, Manager tools, event mesh, scheduler, approvals, artifacts, work resources, Hermes skills, MCP tools, terminal/files/browser/computer use, document/image/data processing, delegation, and web/SMS/review surfaces.

## Canonical pricing

### Free infrastructure

- $0
- one employee for one business
- real broad capability, not a crippled demo
- 1.5 million model-token hard limit per day
- bounded browser use, messaging, concurrency, schedules, and event volume
- standard priority
- no SLA

### Managed employee

- minimum $400 per month
- higher capacity, priority, and concurrency
- managed OAuth, execution, recovery, scheduled work, and event-driven work
- broader browser and computer use
- commercial support and reliability

### Workforce

- $800+ per month or custom
- multiple employees, locations, roles, approval structures, and higher volume

There is no $29-$99 tier. AMTECH is using a free infrastructure wedge and an aggressive managed-service floor, not competing as another low-cost assistant subscription.

## Default capability envelope

The default employee should be capable enough to replace fragmented CRM, estimating, operations, and Zapier-like automation work where the business process is computer-mediated. Capability is constrained by scoped identity, policy, approval gates, budgets, concurrency, controlled egress, and evidence requirements rather than by stripping the employee of useful tools.

## Vertical generation

Vertical packages define vocabulary, business facts, default workflows, approval policies, connector defaults, artifacts, and operating procedures. They do not fork the platform. Painting is the first package and proof environment; the canonical product remains a broad AI employee generated conversationally for a specific business.

## Contractor lead-to-cash lifecycle

The initial end-to-end lifecycle is:

1. ingest a lead or customer event from email, web, SMS, files, or a connected system;
2. qualify and enrich it using the business brain;
3. schedule or coordinate the next action;
4. prepare estimates, proposals, files, and customer communication;
5. request owner approval only for consequential actions;
6. send, follow up, and update operational state;
7. collect deposits or payments through Stripe;
8. synchronize bookkeeping evidence through QuickBooks;
9. preserve artifacts, proof, and durable business memory.

## Infrastructure advantage

Hermes supplies a strong agent substrate: terminal, filesystem, browser, computer use, delegation, skills, and MCP. AMTECH adds production boundaries, continuous business identity, connected systems, approval policy, durable events, recovery, work materialization, metering, and a managed operating model.

The advantage is not a larger list of tools. It is the combination of broad tools with business context, scoped authority, durable execution, proof, and recovery.

## Subsidy economics and abuse controls

The free tier is a customer-acquisition and infrastructure-learning subsidy. It must have enforceable daily model-token limits, bounded browser and message volume, concurrency caps, schedule caps, account and employee identity binding, anti-automation abuse detection, revocation, and centralized metering. Expensive or high-risk browser/computer procedures may require managed status, explicit approval, or lower quotas.

## Product presentation

The website should present the employee as a near-real, high-quality AI-generated person with a name, role, presence, and visible work output. The 11x Alice landing-page concept is a useful category lesson: buyers understand an employee more quickly when the hero represents a specific worker rather than an abstract automation diagram. AMTECH should develop an original visual identity and composition rather than copying 11x creative assets or page design.

```text
-> provisioned isolated Hermes runtime
-> owner Work Surface
-> connected business capabilities
```
