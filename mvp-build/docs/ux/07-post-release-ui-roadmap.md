# Release-Adjacent And Post-Release UI Roadmap

Status: active roadmap  
Updated: 2026-07-19  
Purpose: organize UI-focused work around and after the first normal-employee release

## Sequence Rule

The canonical normal-employee production gate comes first. The first provider-backed generative work object is the immediate release-adjacent UX acceptance slice after that gate. Broader manipulation, mobile, public, learned adaptation, and visual-system expansion follow proven work and proof paths.

The speculative research disposition in `08-speculative-ui-research-disposition.md` controls what may enter each track. Research metaphors do not become runtime architecture without a bounded contract, measurable user benefit, accessibility proof, and exact-SHA evidence.

## Tuesday Closure Track

Spend the remaining UI budget on release evidence and standing defects:

- production-build browser matrix rather than development-server-only screenshots;
- deterministic layout and focus across input ordering;
- explicit priority and rationale for high-risk decisions, returned active saves, blocked work, and material delegated failure;
- 44px critical controls, unobscured focus, keyboard operation, reduced motion, and mobile overflow checks;
- research, blocked/degraded, and signed-review fixture coverage;
- immutable source, browser, accessibility, and layout reports against the exact SHA;
- no regression to fixture-free normal-employee deployment, assignment, approval, C3, receipt, or revocation semantics.

## Track 1: Trust And Proof

- Proof search and filters by customer, job, action, date, and connected account.
- Proof detail pages that show what Avery did, what the owner approved, and what the external system confirmed.
- Better failure, ambiguity, reconciliation, and repair states when connectors or provider calls fail.
- Owner-readable layout rationale and context provenance without private reasoning.

## Track 2: Generative Work Objects

- Live provider-backed generated tables, forms, schedules, diffs, and media review surfaces.
- Stronger visual design for generated UI, aligned to the light Avery system.
- Owner-readable explanation of why each surface appeared and what action follows.
- Object-level action history and proof.
- Typed, registered component selection only; no arbitrary model-authored DOM.

## Track 3: Direct Manipulation And Forgiveness

- Attach customer inputs to jobs.
- Reorder estimate sections.
- Compare versions.
- Drag/drop documents or media into a work object.
- Convert a proof or customer reply into a follow-up task.
- Add bounded undo/revert where the external system and receipt model support it.

## Track 4: Mobile Job-Site Use

- Fast voice/text capture.
- Photo and document intake.
- Offline-tolerant drafts.
- One-handed approval/reply flows.
- Explicit interrupted/reconnecting state that never silently replays a command.

## Track 5: Public And Customer Surfaces

- Canonical public create/claim/login/account onboarding surfaces.
- Customer estimate portal.
- Public website work demonstrations and marketing materialization.
- Visitor-session isolation and deterministic HTML-to-PDF.

The public estimator is outdated and non-canonical. It may remain as a separated acquisition or regression harness, but it is not a future flagship product surface and must not drive this roadmap.

## Track 6: Visual System And Accessibility

- Apply the Avery-first light system across public, admin, billing, portal, artifacts, and generated UI.
- Run contrast, keyboard, focus, target-size, reduced-motion, zoom/reflow, and screen-reader audits.
- Define icon, motion, layout, and typography rules that future agents can implement without guessing.
- Preserve stable landmarks and user-selected density during adaptation.

## Track 7: Adaptive UI Research

Only after the normal-employee and first generative-work-object gates:

- compare authored and adaptive region ordering in controlled task studies;
- persist explicit owner-selected density and explanation preferences;
- evaluate task success, error rate, decision comprehension, recovery, time-to-proof, and trust calibration;
- experiment with bounded component ordering among equal-priority items;
- require a stable baseline, escape hatch, explanation, rollback, and reduced-motion behavior for every experiment.

Do not pursue emotion recognition, personality inference, fatigue/vulnerability scoring, cursor surveillance, chain-of-thought inspection, WebGPU reaction-diffusion UI, gradient-flow navigation, or opaque reinforcement-learning layout control as product features. Those concepts remain rejected or research-only until a separate safety, legal, scientific, and accessibility review changes this record.
