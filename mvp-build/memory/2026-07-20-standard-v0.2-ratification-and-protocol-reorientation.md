## Purpose and invariant

Ratify the original AMTECH Standard without discarding its core AI-labor ontology; correct outdated or overbroad assumptions through current research and source evidence; restore connector-agnostic/native authorization direction; define a database TDD inner loop that does not depend on constant live database access; and consolidate scattered planning/document authority.

## Step-back result

The original Standard's central direction remains correct:

- employee identity is not tenant ownership;
- assignment is execution scope;
- human relationships and commercial roles are separate;
- durable work, approval, effect, receipt, repair, and proof are required;
- Hermes is runtime and Manager is authority/custody;
- evidence states cannot be collapsed.

The correct motion was primarily `satisfaction` and `reorientation`, not destructive replacement.

Material reorientations:

- official MCP Apps replaces generic “MCP-UI” as the interoperability target;
- AG-UI is an optional agent-user event/state adapter, not a generated-UI or authority protocol;
- OAuth security follows current RFC/MCP profiles, while provider-hosted onboarding, service accounts, and managed secrets are represented honestly;
- standard in-toto/SLSA attestations are the supply-chain interoperability target;
- local/CI PostgreSQL is the TDD loop and disposable Supabase is a material platform/release gate.
