# Visual System

Status: active  
Purpose: define the emotional and visual direction

## Emotional Target

The product should feel:

- calm;
- warm;
- capable;
- dimensional;
- trustworthy;
- quietly branded;
- obviously interactive.

It should not feel:

- harsh;
- operational;
- generic SaaS;
- AI-gradient trendy;
- compliance-heavy;
- like a CRM;
- like an admin console.

## Aqua Translation

Use Aqua as emotional reference, not visual costume.

Carry forward:

- depth shows hierarchy;
- translucency can make layered sheets feel natural;
- controls should look touchable;
- motion can explain where a thing came from and where it went;
- warmth and delight reduce fear around new computing models.

Do not carry forward:

- literal glossy candy buttons;
- blue gel styling;
- skeuomorphic clutter;
- chrome-heavy windows;
- decorative reflections;
- low-contrast glass behind important text.

## Color

Palette intent:

- warm off-white background;
- deep charcoal text;
- soft blue or teal for Avery presence and calm capability;
- green for completed proof;
- amber for owner attention;
- red only for destructive/high-risk/failure states;
- restrained AMTECH red as a brand accent, never the dominant field.

Avoid:

- purple/blue AI haze;
- red-dominant styling;
- beige-only palettes;
- dark slate SaaS dashboards;
- one-note monochrome systems.

## Typography

Primary: modern humanist sans, preferably Inter or a similar already available system-compatible stack.

Use IBM Plex Mono only for:

- compact proof IDs in admin;
- diagnostic snippets;
- optional receipt metadata.

Do not use mono as the product voice. It made earlier designs feel operational and cold.

Typography rules:

- hero-scale type is not for the app interior.
- Home uses quiet medium scale.
- Approval details must be readable at mobile distance.
- Dense labels should be short, not tiny.
- Letter spacing remains normal.

## Shape And Depth

Use depth to explain interaction:

- Home surface is soft and quiet.
- Composer is the clearest affordance.
- Approval sheets sit above the product with a subtle material layer.
- Work objects have tactile edges.
- Proof receipts are flatter and quieter.

Cards are allowed for active objects and repeated items. Do not place cards inside cards. Page sections are not
floating card stacks.

## Motion

Motion should explain state:

- Avery prepared something -> item settles into Needs your say.
- Approval resolved -> sheet closes into proof receipt.
- Blocked connection -> Connected opens from the blocker.
- Tweak -> returns to Talk with selected object context.

Avoid:

- decorative animation loops;
- constant typing indicators;
- fake autonomous activity;
- motion that competes with approval reading.

Respect reduced-motion preferences.

## Icons And Controls

Use familiar icons where they reduce reading:

- send;
- microphone;
- check;
- x/decline;
- clock;
- link/connected;
- receipt;
- file;
- lock.

Buttons should name business commands where stakes are high:

- Approve send
- Approve invoice
- Reply
- Tweak
- Decline

## Brand Expression

AMTECH brand should come from:

- confident simplicity;
- warm precision;
- small red accents;
- Avery's employee presence;
- proof/safety polish;
- dimensional interaction.

Not from:

- giant marketing headline in the app;
- badge-heavy brand labels;
- red-black severity styling;
- AI vapor gradients.
