**Status: active** · _Created 2026-07-12. Current as of 2026-07-12 web/vendor checks plus the 2026-07-11/12 AMTECH product state in `README.md`, `wiki/MVP/second-half-current-and-future-state.md`, and `mvp-build/memory/MEMORY.md`._

## AMTECH lens

AMTECH is not just an estimate generator. The current product is a managed, textable AI Employee for owner-led SMBs: one employee relationship, one number/thread, web Work Surface + SMS ambient inbox + signed mobile Review, business-brain context, Manager/MCP tool control, typed work resources, connector events, and structural approval gates for customer-facing or money-touching actions. The contractor estimate loop is the proof object: job notes/photos/email context -> line-item estimate -> owner approval -> Gmail send/reply -> deposit invoice/payment/reminder.

The current state matters for competition:

- **Beachhead now:** owner-operated residential painting and landscaping/hardscaping contractors, 1-10 person shops, especially phone-reachable owners who write estimates and follow-up at night.
- **5-year customer base:** owner-led SMBs supervising vertical AI Employees across connected back-office work, not isolated apps.
- **Product edge:** AMTECH owns the packaged employee, trust/approval UX, business brain, connector custody, typed work surface, and operator/factory layer over Hermes. Raw AI capability is not the moat.
- **Current build reality:** Work Surface, SMS review links, Connector Center/resurfacing, Gmail/Stripe/QuickBooks connectors, MCP-UI cards, admin/operator surfaces, and container orchestration are `source-wired`; live provider/runtime proof and real-VPS proof are still pending.

## Strategic takeaways

1. **The actual direct battle is painting/home-service workflow software, not generic AI.** QuoteIQ, PaintScout, DripJobs, Jobber, and Housecall Pro own the comparison frame a contractor already understands.
2. **The actual indirect battle is "good enough office relief."** AI receptionists, local automation shops, and contractor answering services can absorb urgency before AMTECH demonstrates the estimate-to-cash loop.
3. **QuickBooks is a strategic watch item.** AMTECH's QuickBooks connector makes QBO a capability inside the Employee, but Intuit's agentic AI direction makes it a possible direct competitor for invoices, bills, payments, and estimates.
4. **AMTECH's sales language should avoid commodity AI automation.** The defensible phrase is not "AI tools for contractors"; it is "a textable employee that prepares your estimate in your pricing, shows the proof, asks before sending, watches the reply, and keeps the job moving."
5. **The product bar must stay ahead of suite AI.** Web/SMS surfaces, signed approvals, typed resources, Connector Center, business memory, and live provider proof are not internal niceties; they are how AMTECH stays different when field-service platforms add AI features.
