# The Entrepreneurship Playbook — Building Out the Beachhead

**Status: complete** · _Created 2026-06-27. The craft layer: how the paint/landscape beachhead gets built out, how to find the winning way to attain a client, and how this bootstraps to $10M+. Grounded in Aulet, Paul Graham, Blank, Moore, and Hormozi._

## The fixed course vs. the open question

Two things must be held apart:

- **The course is fixed** (do not re-litigate it): sell installed AI office workers to owner-operated SMBs; **beachhead = paint/landscape contractors**; ladder = Rung 0 consultative first work → Rung 1 tuned Estimate package ($300 paid discovery) → Rung 2 live AI Employee → Rung 3 managed office loop (the skill is the proof object + supply chain; the Employee is the product); positioning = the commercialization layer on improving intelligence, pro-human; operating model = maximize agent leverage.
- **The open question is the craft:** *how exactly do you attain a client, and how do you build out every aspect of the beachhead?* This is unknown and must be discovered empirically — Blank's "there are no facts inside the building" [S071]. The rest of this page is the method for that search.

### Paul Graham
- **Schlep blindness** [S069]: the contractor back-office grind — estimates at 9pm, chasing payments, sorting receipts — is the *ugly, important work everyone avoids*. That is precisely why it's a defensible business and why incumbents and AI-native founders skip it. **Run toward the schlep.**
- **Do things that don't scale** [S063]: hand-deliver the first estimates, do the 20-minute interviews yourself, recruit clients one call at a time. The manual phase is how you learn what to automate.
- **Default alive** [S064]: track weekly whether revenue covers expenses without outside money. With ~97% margins, default-alive is close — protect it.
- **Make something people want** [S070]: the demo on the contractor's *own* job is the "people want" test, live.

### Steve Blank — customer development [S071]
Discovery → Validation → Creation → Company Building, and **expect several iterations of each.** You are in Discovery/Validation: get out of the building, talk to contractors, let the live demo test the hypothesis. "It's OK to screw it up if you plan to learn from it."

### Geoffrey Moore — the bowling pin [S073]
Dominate one narrow segment completely, *then* expand to **adjacent** segments from a stronger position. Paint → landscape → adjacent trades (roofing, concrete, fencing, remodeling) → desk-based offices (bookkeepers). Each pin is easier because the platform, the profile factory, and the proof carry over. **You vary the segment only after you own the current one — never to escape difficulty.**

### Hormozi — Core Four & volume [S072]
The menu of ways to get clients (warm / content / cold / ads) and the discipline of **~100 primary actions/day, "volume negates luck."** The agent-run conveyor is what makes that volume survivable for one person.

## The method: iterative vs. lateral search

This is the heart of the ask — how to find the best way to attain a client **without ever changing course.** Treat client-acquisition as an experiment with two move-types:

| | **Iterative (vertical)** | **Lateral (horizontal)** |
|---|---|---|
| What you change | One variable inside the *same* play — list, opener, demo, offer, timing | The *play itself* — a different Core Four channel × offer |
| When to use | While each change still moves the metric | When the current play plateaus below target |
| Canon | Blank "iterate each step"; PG "do things that don't scale to learn" | Hormozi Core Four as the menu; still one beachhead (Moore) |
| Example | Cold-call opener v1 → v2 → v3; demo on their own job | Cold call → free teardown → referral loop → supply-house |

**Decision rule.** Keep iterating while iterations move the number and you're above a viable floor. **Go lateral** when either: (a) the play has plateaued for **two cycles** at a conversion that can't hit the cash target even at max volume, or (b) it hits a structural wall (e.g., owners unreachable midday). **Never abandon the course — only abandon plays.** A failing opener is an iteration; a failing channel is a lateral move; a failing *segment* is not on the table until paint/landscape is won.

**Cadence.** One named experiment per week, logged in the Friday review ([`founder-52-week-operating-plan.md`](founder-52-week-operating-plan.md)): hypothesis → action → metric → keep/kill → next. The agent conveyor ([`gtm/outbound-system.md`](gtm/outbound-system.md)) makes lateral tests cheap, so you can search the play-space fast while the founder's scarce hours stay on demos and closes.

## The bridge to $10M+

Revenue = **clients × lanes-per-client × price × retention**, at person-minimal margin. Each term has a named lever:

| Lever | Driver | Page |
|---|---|---|
| Lanes-per-client (ARPU) | the skill catalog | [`offers/skill-catalog.md`](offers/skill-catalog.md) |
| Retention | Employee stickiness + intelligence curve + installed context | [`strategy-4-year-implications.md`](strategy-4-year-implications.md) |
| Client volume | agent-run outbound (no labor ceiling) | [`principle-agent-leverage.md`](principle-agent-leverage.md) |
| Replication | bowling-pin to adjacent trades + bookkeepers | this page |
| Margin | ~97%, person-minimal | [`principle-agent-leverage.md`](principle-agent-leverage.md) |

A rough shape of $10M ARR: **~550 accounts at ~$1,500/mo**, or a mix of tuned packages and recurring Employees across paint/landscape + two adjacent pins. What makes that reachable near-solo is that **the conveyor and the profile factory do the work** — the founder is the trust layer, not the labor.

**The innovation-commercialization thesis:** the "best parts" Ben extracts from this bootstrap are not the painting niche — they are **the repeatable vertical-profile factory and the agent-run go-to-market.** Those *become* the company. The contractor business is the proving ground where AMTECH learns to commercialize agent intelligence into installed SMB workers — the thing that scales to a real startup.

## The one-page loop

1. Pick the beachhead persona's next 10 (Aulet theme 1).
2. Run the warmest acquisition play first (Hormozi); agent-prep it (leverage principle).
3. Run the live surface on their own job (PG "make something people want"); provision the Employee; close the tuned package.
4. Deepen lanes/deliverable types (skill catalog) as each loop is felt — the progressive-trust upsell.
5. Weekly: iterate the play, or go lateral if it capped (the method).
6. When paint/landscape is owned: roll to the next pin (Moore).
7. Productize every repeated step into a skill, a profile, or an agent — never reset to zero.
