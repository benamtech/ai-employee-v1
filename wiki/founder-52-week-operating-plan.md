# Founder 52-Week Operating Plan

**Status: complete** · _Revised 2026-07-14 for the current motion: the free public estimator is the first contractor wedge; the Work Surface and live AI Employee are the product; the Estimate skill is the first proof object and the supply chain. See [`00-decision.md`](00-decision.md), [`gtm/free-estimator-funnel.md`](gtm/free-estimator-funnel.md), [`offers/wedge-offers.md`](offers/wedge-offers.md), and the MVP build state in [`MVP/implementation-records/`](MVP/implementation-records/)._

## Operating premise

AMTECH is a bootstrapped startup, not a funded company and not a lifestyle agency. The first year must do two things at once:

1. **Make cash fast** by getting owners into a real work loop through the free public estimator — one real job, one line-item estimate draft, then sell the **tuned Estimate package (paid discovery)** and the **live AI Employee (setup + monthly)**. The buyer already has the pain; the estimator lets them feel the product before a sales call.
2. **Compound product value** by turning every sale into a better Hermes profile package, a tighter vertical skill, a reusable onboarding/consulting script, more deliverable types on the Work Surface, and a referenceable proof asset — and by riding the intelligence curve instead of hand-building.

**Beachhead: owner-operated residential painting & landscaping/hardscaping contractors (1–10 person shops).** The ladder is the offer: **Rung 0 free public estimator → Rung 1 tuned Estimate package ($300-class paid discovery) → Rung 2 live AI Employee ($750 setup + $1,000/mo Starter / $1,500/mo Pro) → Rung 3 managed office loop.** The skill is the proof object and the supply chain; the **Employee relationship is the product.** Beachhead #2 (bookkeepers/desk-based offices) is a sequenced fast-follow, not a parallel bet. Full rationale: [`00-decision.md`](00-decision.md); funnel mechanics: [`gtm/free-estimator-funnel.md`](gtm/free-estimator-funnel.md).

### The product is largely built — so Year 1 is acceptance + selling, not building from scratch

The MVP is no longer a slide and a hand-run skill. `mvp-build/` already has the front-door onboarding orchestrator, account/claim/provisioning, the live employee over SMS + web, the estimate artifact + signed link + approval, Gmail send/reply (source-complete), Stripe test-mode deposit invoice, reminders, and the typed Work Surface. Current state and the exact provider-acceptance gaps are in [`MVP/implementation-records/2026-06-29-phase-3-partial-record.md`](MVP/implementation-records/2026-06-29-phase-3-partial-record.md). **The Year-1 build job is to reach real provider acceptance on the whole loop and harden it under real pilots — not to invent the demo.** The demo *is* the onboarding surface producing a real work object on the owner's own job.

### The thesis that governs what you build vs. rent

AMTECH is the **commercialization layer on improving agent intelligence** (Hermes/Nous + frontier models). The deliverable gets better on its own as the substrate improves. So:

- **Invest only in the durable layer:** vertical profile packages, business-brain capture, connectors, the typed deliverable/approval grammar, the Work Surface, customer relationships, the skill supply chain.
- **Rent the intelligence.** Don't hand-engineer capability the next model will do natively for free. Stay model-agnostic so you can swap the brain underneath and absorb model drift *for* the owner.
- **Get installed early.** The value is the packaging + the loaded context; provision the Employee early so customers accumulate their business brain and ride the curve from inside. The MVP provisions free-by-default, which makes "install first, monetize the loop" the natural motion.

### Operating doctrine (from the research)

- Do things that don't scale: manually recruit users, deliver concierge value, learn from early customers [S063].
- Become default alive: track whether current revenue/expenses survive without outside capital [S064].
- Use early service revenue as a runway tool, not the destination [S065].
- Stay on one beachhead until the data forces a switch [S066].
- Founder does the sales, charges early, tracks a funnel, works backward from customer targets [S067].

How to choose and switch acquisition plays each week (iterative vs lateral), and which steps to hand to agents: [`entrepreneurship-playbook.md`](entrepreneurship-playbook.md), [`gtm/outbound-system.md`](gtm/outbound-system.md), and [`principle-agent-leverage.md`](principle-agent-leverage.md).

## Non-negotiable rules

- Work 30–50 hours/week; minimum 30 means no optional work before the sales quota is hit.
- **No second vertical** until at least 100 contractor conversations or 10 paid attempts are completed.
- No unpaid open-ended pilots. The tuned package is paid; the Employee is paid setup + monthly (a time-boxed beta/case-study price is fine). Never free open-ended builds.
- No custom consulting language. Every job becomes a reusable skill, profile package, deliverable type, checklist, or script.
- No cold texting. Phone + email outbound only; anything leaving a customer's business stays behind a one-tap approval (TCPA).
- **Don't hand-build what the model will soon do for free.** Spend the time on context capture, the trust/approval UX, the Work Surface, and relationships.
- **Sell the Employee relationship, not a one-off skill.** The tuned package is paid discovery that installs the business brain; never pitch "buy a skill, maybe an Employee someday."
- Every Friday: update cash, pipeline, calls, work objects produced, Employees provisioned, MRR, churn risk, and what was learned.

## Annual revenue targets

Operating targets, not promises.

## Weekly time budget (40-hour week)

- 18 hrs: prospecting, estimator-attempt follow-up, guided calls, closing.
- 8 hrs: paid delivery (tuned packages, Employee provisioning) and customer success.
- 8 hrs: productizing into the `contractor_estimator` profile package + Work Surface deliverable types; reaching/holding provider acceptance.
- 3 hrs: proof assets (before/after estimates, short clips, real artifacts).
- 3 hrs: admin, metrics, finance, planning.

At 30 hrs, cut proof/admin first, never sales. At 50 hrs, add to prospecting and delivery.

## Daily default schedule

- **Mon:** enrich/rank the list; first-touch calls to no-website rows; book demos.
- **Tue:** follow-up calls/emails; run the onboarding-surface demo on real jobs; provision Employees.
- **Wed:** more outbound; in-person demos where local; produce real estimate artifacts.
- **Thu:** close open deals; sell tuned packages + Employee setups; ask for referrals.
- **Fri:** finish delivery; update metrics; turn one delivery lesson into a reusable profile/deliverable improvement; plan next week's experiment.
- **Weekend:** optional 2–4 hrs only for delivery/sales prep. No speculative features.

## Funnel metrics (track weekly in one sheet)

new prospects enriched · emails sent · calls made · estimator starts · completed drafts · downloads · feedback · **real work objects produced** · tuned packages sold · **Employees provisioned** · revenue collected · MRR · provider-proof loops completed · delivery hours · reusable assets/deliverable types created · referrals asked/received · biggest objection · best customer quote · what was learned.

Minimum weekly outbound baseline after Week 1: **60 targeted estimator emails/follow-ups, 30 calls, 8 completed estimator attempts reviewed, 5 referral asks.**

## Weeks 1–4 — first cash off the list you already have

You hold ~150 phone-only (no-website) contractors and ~200 with websites. **Step 1 is enrichment, not list-building.** Seasonality is in your favor: late June is peak paint/landscape estimate season.

### Week 1 — get the surface acceptance-ready, work the no-website list

- Tag every painter/landscaper/hardscaper in the ~350; flag with-website rows as **estimator-email-first** and no-website phone rows as **guided-estimator-call-first**.
- **Make the close-able demo the real onboarding surface:** drive the front-door flow on 3 real sample jobs and produce clean **line-item** estimate artifacts with the approval card. Frame generic output as *pre-tuning*. Close any open provider-acceptance gaps blocking a believable live run ([implementation record](MVP/implementation-records/2026-06-29-phase-3-partial-record.md)).
- Send the free-estimator email to with-website rows and call 30 no-website contractors early morning/evening. Pitch the *result*, not the tech: "we give contractors one free estimate from a real job."
- Offer Rung 0 (free estimator attempt on a job they pick) -> book the tuned-package interview or Employee setup after the draft creates trust.
- Tracking sheet live.
- **Kill criterion:** if no demos booked, narrow the trade (lead painters only) and tighten the opener to one sentence about night-time estimates.

### Week 2 — close the first tuned packages + provision the first Employees

- Add/enrich 60 more rows; 60 calls; 40 emails to website rows ("first work from your own recent job").
- Run ≥5 live onboarding-surface demos. On each: show the line-item draft, the approval gate, and the next action the Employee would take; ask "is that within a few percent of what you'd charge?" When yes → provision the Employee and "$300 to tune it to your exact pricing so it becomes your Employee's pricing brain, back in 48 hours."
- Collect via payment link **before** the build. Deliver in 48 hrs with one revision.
- **Decision:** if 1–2 pay, keep the offer. If they love it but don't pay, the demo isn't on *their* job — fix that. If nobody cares, test landscaping vs painting split.

### Week 3 — turn delivery into repeatable proof

- Deliver tuned packages with excessive care; capture before/after (their hand estimate vs. the Employee's artifact) with permission.
- Get one-sentence testimonials ("it nailed it within X%, saved me an evening").
- 60 calls, 40 emails.
- **Productize:** improve the `contractor_estimator` package + the 20-minute interview script (markup %, labor/crew costs, materials/suppliers, standard assemblies, service area, format) — every interview feeds the reusable package, not a one-off.

### Week 4 — find the fastest-reacting sub-trade

- Track response by: interior painters, exterior painters, landscaping (maintenance), hardscape/install.
- Double down on the best reactor. 70 calls, 50 emails.
- Close 3–5 more tuned packages; provision Employees for the willing.
- **End-of-month target:** $1,000–$3,000 collected; 6–10 tuned packages; 1–3 Employees provisioned; 2–3 testimonials.

## Weeks 5–8 — convert first work into the recurring Employee

### Week 5 — make the ladder easy to buy
Package three fixed options on a one-page sheet: tuned Estimate package ($300) · $750 setup + $1,000/mo Employee (Starter) · $750 setup + $1,500/mo Employee (Pro). 75 emails, 60 calls. Ask every tuned-package buyer for referrals.

### Week 6 — land the first paid recurring Employee
Offer to a happy first-work buyer. Trigger line: "Want it to just *do* this — voice-memo the job, it pulls the thread, drafts the estimate, you approve, it sends and watches the reply?" Provision the Hermes profile + SMS number + **Gmail connector**. Keep scope to estimate → send → reply at first. $750 setup or $500 beta setup for a case study.

### Week 7 — prove the whole loop on real rails
Take one pilot all the way through **provider-accepted** golden path: estimate artifact → approved Gmail send → real reply event → approved Stripe test-mode deposit invoice → reminder. This is the Year-1 proof that the product is real, not a mock. Delete any provisioning step that can't be reused; fold it into the profile factory.

### Week 8 — first real review
Calls, demos, tuned-package conversion, Employees provisioned, avg delivery hours, gross revenue, MRR, best sub-trade, objections, whether the Employee trigger landed, **whether a full provider-accepted loop ran**. **Decision:** ≥6 paid customers and 1 accepted loop → stay the course. 0 paid after 150+ touches and 15+ demos → narrow trade or fix the demo; do **not** jump to bookkeepers yet.

## Weeks 9–13 — build the repeatable sales engine

- **W9:** standard CRM sheet + scripts (call opener, surface demo script, tuned-package close, Employee trigger, objection responses). 75 prospects, 60 emails, 60 calls, 6 demos.
- **W10:** one public proof asset — "watch a painter's estimate get written, sent, and the reply handled" (sanitized real artifacts + call link).
- **W11:** referral push. Ask every contact and customer for one painter/landscaper name; $100 credit only after a paid close.
- **W12:** local live-demo push — offer 5 in-person "bring your last estimate, I'll set up your employee on it" slots (your local advantage). First work free; tuning + Employee paid.
- **W13 — quarter checkpoint:** target $5k–$10k collected or $2k–$3k recurring, and ≥1 provider-accepted loop. If under $2k, diagnose list quality / demo-on-their-job / call volume / price / sub-trade before any new build.

## Weeks 14–26 — turn paid work into MRR

- **W14:** harden `contractor_estimator` — onboarding intake mapping, memory seed, estimate deliverable type, approval rules, two check-ins, output examples; lock the provider-accepted loop.
- **W15:** sell recurring Employees at $750 setup + $1,000/mo (or $500 beta + $1,000/mo for a case study). First-work buyers first.
- **W16:** stabilize delivery — document every manual provisioning step, turn repeats into the profile factory, track support time, audit approval gates on anything outbound/money.
- **W17:** outbound theme "stop estimating at 9pm." Target shops visibly slammed in peak season.
- **W18:** second proof asset — case study on evenings saved + bids won + a real deposit collected (don't overclaim revenue).
- **W19:** tighten the demo to 7 minutes — voice memo → email pull → line-item draft → approval → send. Stop explaining Hermes unless asked.
- **W20:** turn on the **Drive connector / Pro tier** ($1,500/mo): past estimates/pricing/photos → zero manual context. Upsell Starter accounts.
- **W21:** customer-success sprint — call every paying customer; ask what they'd pay monthly to never write an estimate manually again, and which next office loop hurts most.
- **W22:** add the **deposit-invoice + reminder lane** to Pro (Stripe deposit from approved estimate, reminder on accepted job, behind the gate).
- **W23:** bookkeeping backup experiment **only if** the contractor pipeline is weak — otherwise skip. If run: build the doc-sorter skill, 25 prospects, 10 calls, 5 demos.
- **W24:** profile hardening — validation, delivery checklist, onboarding checklist, support boundaries, a "what the Employee will not do" page.
- **W25:** MRR push — offer the Employee to every tuned-package buyer who hasn't provisioned a paid account.
- **W26 — midyear review:** target 4–6 recurring Employees and the tuned package running as an automated paid-discovery step. Commit to contractors through W39 if working; switch to bookkeepers only with evidence, not boredom.

## Weeks 27–39 — productize and scale the winning motion

- **W27:** name and package the offer — `Your AI Office` landing page, one real artifact set, one short clip, one price sheet.
- **W28:** raise outbound volume to 100 prospects / 75 emails / 75 calls / 8 demos weekly.
- **W29:** small "estimate teardown" lead magnet — free first-work score on a real estimate, paid tuning + Employee.
- **W30:** quarterly package — $1,500 setup + $1,500/mo, 90-day minimum, for buyers who've already felt the loop.
- **W31:** proof week — testimonials, anonymized clips, real provider-proofed artifacts, referrals, publish proof.
- **W32:** process cleanup — drop low-margin one-off requests; write "not included" terms; cancellation/support boundaries.
- **W33:** channel test — 10 contractors who'd refer peers; capture referral language; still close direct first.
- **W34:** build the second Employee lane as a **deliverable type** — customer follow-up + job folder (still no cold outbound).
- **W35:** pricing test — quote $1,500 setup + $1,500/mo to new prospects; track close-rate impact.
- **W36:** operational audit — support hrs/account, delivery hrs/account, gross margin, failure cases, approval-gate gaps, provider-event reliability.
- **W37:** "first 30 days with your AI Office" onboarding plan.
- **W38:** community credibility — useful estimate/ops content in contractor groups; no spam.
- **W39 — quarter review:** target 8–10 recurring accounts or a clear path. If first work sells but recurring lags, fix the Employee onboarding/trust loop — do not retreat to selling detached skills.

## Weeks 40–52 — make it a business, not a hustle

- **W40:** annual dashboard — MRR, cash, pipeline, churn risk, productized deliverable types, support load, profile-package versions.
- **W41:** push quarterly prepay (10% discount); no annual contracts until delivery is stable.
- **W42:** v1 docs — onboarding, profile-package generation, deliverable-type catalog, support rules, incident handling, weekly check-in template.
- **W43:** hire nothing unless MRR > $8k, delivery is repeatable, the role is obvious, and the work is documented. If hiring, start with part-time delivery/research help, not sales.
- **W44:** open **beachhead #2 (bookkeepers)** in earnest if contractor motion is stable — the same platform + Work Surface, the doc-sorter skill now built and tested.
- **W45:** profile-library roadmap — `contractor_estimator`, `contractor_office_admin`, `bookkeeping_doc_coordinator`, then adjacent trades/offices. Build the next only after the current package has paid retention.
- **W46:** partner beta — 3 contractors max; simple referral terms; AMTECH keeps delivery.
- **W47:** year-end customer interviews — what did it save, what did it replace, what still feels manual, what would make it indispensable.
- **W48:** raise prices for new customers if retention is good ($1,500–2,500 setup; $1,500–2,500/mo by lane count).
- **W49:** annual case study — one customer, one workflow, before/after, real provider-proofed artifacts, no vague AI claims.
- **W50:** reliability sprint — audit approval gates, SMS/Twilio flow, Gmail/Stripe event reliability, profile generation, data handling, support logs, **and the model-swap path** (can you change the brain underneath without customers noticing?).
- **W51:** decide next-year strategy — double down on contractors / scale bookkeepers / add adjacent trades / build channel / deepen the Work Surface. From revenue and retention, not curiosity.
- **W52:** reset — total revenue, MRR, churn, gross margin, hourly earnings, best channel, best offer. Kill weak offers; keep one core profile package; set next year's target.

## First 14 days in detail

- **Day 1:** enrich 40 rows (paint/landscape); write one call opener; run the onboarding surface on 1 real sample job; call 20 no-website rows.
- **Day 2:** enrich 20 more; 20 calls; 20 emails to website rows; produce a second sample estimate artifact.
- **Day 3:** follow up all opens/replies; 20 calls; offer the free first work + tuned package explicitly.
- **Day 4:** run demos (in person where local); 20 calls; provision Employees for the willing; book the 20-min tuning interviews.
- **Day 5:** send payment links; collect; deliver any tuned package in 48 hrs.
- **Day 6:** finish delivery; capture before/after; ask for a testimonial; document repeatable steps.
- **Day 7:** review numbers; rewrite weak scripts; plan next 60 rows.
- **Days 8–14:** repeat at double volume. Build no features unless a paid delivery or a provider-acceptance gap requires it.

## What not to do this year

- Don't hand-engineer capability the next model will do for free.
- Don't demo on a generic job — always the contractor's own, in the real onboarding surface.
- **Don't sell a detached skill as the product** — the skill is the proof object; the Employee relationship is the sale.
- Don't run two cold beachheads at once.
- Don't build a dashboard before three customers ask for the same thing — let the deliverable-type system render new work.
- Don't sell autonomous outbound, lead gen, or cold texting.
- Don't claim MVP completion from local mocks — a capability isn't done until it leaves real provider proof.
- Don't accept hourly consulting that can't become a skill/profile/deliverable type.
- Don't perfect the website instead of calling the list you already have.
