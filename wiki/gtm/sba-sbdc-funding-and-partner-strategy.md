# SBA/SBDC Funding And Partner Strategy

**Status: active** · _Created 2026-07-04. Geographic focus: Scranton / Northeastern Pennsylvania first, Philadelphia second._

## Decision

AMTECH should treat the SBA/SBDC ecosystem as **two linked opportunities, in this order**:

1. **AMTECH as the client seeking financing.** Use University of Scranton SBDC, SBA Lender Match, MetroAction, Ben Franklin Technology Partners of Northeastern PA, StartUp NEPA, and IPart/SBIR support to assemble a realistic capital stack for AMTECH's next 6-12 months.
2. **AMTECH as the trusted AI implementation resource.** Build the local, model-neutral, SBDC-safe AI adoption package that advisors can refer to when small businesses ask, "What should I actually do with AI?"

Do not pitch this as "AMTECH gives money to SBA clients." The immediate point is that **AMTECH needs financing** and is itself a plausible SBA/SBDC client. The partner angle helps because it makes AMTECH easier for local resource partners to understand, refer, and eventually contract with.

## Source-of-truth fit

AMTECH does not sell generic AI consulting. Per the product wiki, AMTECH sells a **textable AI Employee** for owner-led SMBs: a business brain, connectors on the owner's accounts, an approval gate for customer-facing or money-moving actions, and a proof trail. The current beachhead is owner-operated contractors, with the first proof loop around estimates, Gmail, Stripe/deposit, reminders, and owner approval.

That maps cleanly to what SBA/SBDC-style AI guidance is starting to require:

- identify business functions where AI can improve operations, productivity, or customer service;
- evaluate risks, privacy, provider terms, and human involvement;
- help small businesses coordinate with third-party AI providers;
- convert broad AI literacy into practical, local implementation.

The wedge is: **"We help Main Street businesses move from AI literacy to a safe first workflow."**

## Current policy context

As of 2026-07-04, the AI for Main Street Act and AI-WISE Act have **passed the House but are not law**. Congress.gov shows both H.R. 5764 and H.R. 5784 were received in the Senate and referred to the Senate Committee on Small Business and Entrepreneurship on 2026-01-26.

The pending AI for Main Street Act would require SBDCs to help small businesses evaluate AI. The AI-WISE Act would require SBA educational resources on AI tools, including model limits, provider coordination, privacy, risk management, human involvement, task identification, and adoption value. Existing America's SBDC AI U already does part of this through Google.org-funded training, AI clinics, and advisor modules.

The opportunity for AMTECH is not to wait for a federal grant. It is to become the local last-mile implementation partner while the network is still forming.

## Financing AMTECH can pursue

### 1. Immediate working-capital path: MetroAction and SBA microloan

Best fit for: runway, productization, local pilots, insurance/legal/accounting, contractor demo costs, Twilio/provider costs, sales materials, and working capital under roughly $50k-$100k.

- **SBA Microloan:** up to $50,000, average about $13,000. Can fund working capital, supplies, furniture, fixtures, machinery, and equipment. Cannot be used for existing debt or real estate. Terms are set by intermediary lenders; SBA says rates generally run 8%-13% and maximum term is seven years.
- **MetroAction:** local NEPA small-business loans from $1,000 to $100,000. Service area includes Lackawanna County. Published eligibility includes credit score above 500, less than 100 employees, less than $3M annual sales, and business location in the service area.

Recommended ask: **$25k-$50k first**, unless the financial model cleanly supports more. Package it as "AI Employee pilot commercialization and working capital," not vague AI R&D.

Required packet:

- 12-month use-of-funds budget;
- current business plan and owner resume;
- contractor beachhead proof from the wiki;
- 3 pilot/customer targets and expected revenue;
- simple repayment model: setup fees, $300 tuned package, monthly Employee revenue, consulting workshops;
- current business bank statements, tax docs if any, personal financial statement, credit explanation if needed.

### 2. Regional innovation capital: Ben Franklin Technology Partners of Northeastern PA

Best fit for: prototype-to-commercialization funding, technical/business support, early-stage AI/software company credibility, and investor introductions.

Ben Franklin NEP explicitly funds innovative technology startups in its 21-county region, including Lackawanna County. Its eligibility page emphasizes innovation, commercialization intent, employment impact, geographic fit, IP/protectability, and founder financial commitment. It also says this is not a small-business grant; there is a payback process.

Recent NEP funding rounds show strong fit:

- 2026 Q3: $1.9M approved for 11 companies, including AI/software companies and a Lackawanna County AI accessibility platform.
- 2026 Q4: $2.3M approved for 16 companies, including Lackawanna County data/compliance software and multiple AI-enabled companies.

Recommended ask: **do not lead with a dollar amount until screened**. Lead with the milestone: "fund 10-20 owner-led SMB pilots, finalize the AI Employee provisioning loop, and create a repeatable SBDC-safe AI adoption workflow."

Positioning:

- AMTECH is not a custom-dev agency;
- AMTECH is a regional AI implementation platform for owner-led SMBs;
- the beachhead is contractor office work, but the public-private angle is broader Main Street AI adoption.

### 3. StartUp NEPA angel path

Best fit for: equity/angel capital after AMTECH has a credible demo, early users, and a simple local economic-development story.

StartUp NEPA is an angel investment fund for start-up and early-stage small businesses in the NEPA region. It was founded by NEPA Alliance and uses a pitch/intake process. Its summary says the fund targets small businesses with growth and return potential and is designed to strengthen the regional entrepreneurial ecosystem.

Recommended ask: treat this as a **post-demo pitch**, not the first application. Use it after SBDC/Ben Franklin/MetroAction feedback tightens the business plan.

### 4. SBA 7(a)

Best fit for: larger working capital or expansion once AMTECH can show repayment capacity.

SBA's 7(a) program allows up to $5M and can be used for working capital, equipment, furniture, supplies, and machinery/equipment installation, including AI-related expenses. SBA says the business must be operating for profit, located in the U.S., small under SBA standards, creditworthy, and able to repay. SBA also notes the borrower applies through a lender, not SBA directly.

Recommended ask: **not the first stop unless a lender is comfortable with early-stage software/service risk**. Use SBA Lender Match after the microloan/MetroAction/SBDC packet is ready.

### 5. SBIR/STTR and IPart

Best fit for: only if AMTECH frames a real technical R&D question, not ordinary product build.

SBA grants are limited. SBA explicitly says it does not provide grants for starting or expanding a business, except limited categories such as scientific R&D, entrepreneurship-support organizations, exporting, and specific programs. SBIR/STTR can be powerful but should not be forced. AMTECH's core product is commercial implementation, not necessarily grant-funded research.

Viable R&D angles to test with IPart:

- safe agentic workflow approval gates for non-technical SMB owners;
- auditable proof trails for AI actions across email/payments/files;
- low-cost AI deployment patterns for rural and Main Street businesses;
- model-neutral business-brain portability and evaluation.

IPart, linked by Ben Franklin NEP, provides proposal help, training/travel awards up to $1,000, and support for PA technology companies seeking SBIR/STTR.

## SBA/SBDC resource strategy

### The actual gap

AI U gives advisors and business owners foundational training. It is broad and valuable, but it is not the same as installing a safe first workflow for a painter, landscaper, bookkeeper, restaurant, or local service shop.

AMTECH should own the local implementation layer:

> "After AI U teaches what AI is, AMTECH helps the owner choose and install the first safe workflow."

### What AMTECH should build for SBDCs

Create a lightweight, model-neutral package that an SBDC can comfortably host or refer:

1. **Workshop:** "AI That Does One Real Job: A Safe First Workflow For Main Street Businesses."
2. **Advisor handout:** AI adoption checklist aligned to AI-WISE themes: use case, data sensitivity, provider terms, privacy, human approval, proof trail, cost, and exit/export plan.
3. **Client diagnostic:** 20-minute intake that ranks 3 business workflows by ROI and risk.
4. **Pilot offer:** one approval-gated workflow, preferably estimate/follow-up/invoice/reminder for service businesses.
5. **Case-study template:** before/after time saved, risk controls, owner approval points, proof artifacts, revenue impact.
6. **Referral language:** "AMTECH is a local AI workflow implementation provider; SBDC/SBA does not endorse products or services."

The material must be **vendor/model neutral** when used in an SBDC setting. The AI-WISE bill text explicitly says SBA materials should not prefer one AI model/tool or one vendor over another. AMTECH can sell its product after the workshop, but the educational surface should teach principles and show a transparent implementation pattern.

### First local targets

1. **University of Scranton SBDC**  
   Covers Bradford, Lackawanna, Monroe, Pike, Susquehanna, Tioga, Wayne, and Wyoming counties. Use them first as AMTECH's financing/business-plan advisor, then ask about leading a local AI workflow workshop.

2. **Temple SBDC**  
   Covers Philadelphia and Montgomery counties. Their page has a visible "Lead A Workshop" partner form. This is the cleanest Philadelphia entry point for a workshop/referral strategy.

3. **NEPA Alliance**  
   Useful for business financing, federal/state grant navigation, APEX government-contracting assistance, and StartUp NEPA. APEX matters if AMTECH later sells AI guidance or implementation into public-sector-funded entrepreneurship programs.

4. **Ben Franklin Technology Partners NEP**  
   Best regional innovation-capital target. Also a credibility partner because they already fund AI/software companies in the region.

5. **Ben Franklin Technology Partners SEP / Philadelphia ecosystem**  
   Best Philadelphia tech-capital and network target. Pair with Temple SBDC, PIDC, and the Chamber/PACT ecosystem after the NEPA base is moving.

## 30-day operating plan

### Week 1: become a strong borrower/applicant

- Create a one-page use-of-funds budget for $25k, $50k, and $100k.
- Prepare the SBDC borrower packet: business plan, current product screenshots/demo, revenue model, pilot pipeline, personal financial docs, and credit notes.
- Book University of Scranton SBDC as a client seeking financing guidance.
- Contact MetroAction about a working-capital loan fit.

### Week 2: open the innovation-capital path

- Submit Ben Franklin NEP funding questionnaire / contact form.
- Ask specifically whether AMTECH should route through Ben Franklin funding, TechCelerator @ Lackawanna County, IPart/SBIR, or StartUp NEPA.
- Draft the 10-pilot commercialization milestone.

### Week 3: build the SBDC-safe resource asset

- Write the workshop deck and advisor handout.
- Make the first workflow demo use a contractor estimate because it matches AMTECH's source-of-truth beachhead.
- Add a second generic example for any SMB: inbox triage -> draft response -> owner approval -> proof trail.

### Week 4: ask for distribution, not endorsement

- Pitch University of Scranton SBDC: "Can I run this as a no-cost educational workshop while I am also working with you as a financing client?"
- Submit Temple SBDC's "Lead A Workshop" form for the Philadelphia version.
- Ask NEPA Alliance/APEX where AI implementation guidance fits in their training calendar or vendor/resource network.
