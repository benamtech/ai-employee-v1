# Segment — Bookkeeping / Accounting / Tax-Prep Practices (Beachhead #2)

**Status: complete** · _Beachhead #2, sequenced behind contractors. See [`../00-decision.md`](../00-decision.md) §10._

Small bookkeeping, CAS, and tax practices are AMTECH's **second beachhead** — the fast-follow once the paint/landscape motion is running. They share the same platform (Hermes + connectors + a vertical skill + business brain); the only difference is the wedge skill, which has to be built first.

## Why #2, not first

Bookkeepers fix the one real contractor weakness — **reachability** (they're at a desk during business hours, numbers-literate, ROI-rational, recurring-client budgets). But contractors lead because AMTECH **already owns** the Estimate skill, so the demo is buildable today; the bookkeeper wedge requires **building a doc-sorter / missing-docs skill first** (a ~week-8–12 project), which is why it's sequenced, not simultaneous. One person, one beachhead until first cash.

## Pain and task shape

Concrete and recurring: clients dump random documents, monthly closes repeat, emails get missed, tax season creates file chaos. Operators describe handling clients who dump documents [S006], a repeatable monthly loop (bank feeds, statements, transaction coding, issue flags) [S007], and "drowning" inside a disorganized firm with email/document-system failures [S008].

Best Skill-shaped tasks:

- **Client document intake sorter** + missing-items request (the wedge to build).
- Month-end close prep pack: pull inputs into a checklist, flag missing docs, draft client follow-up.
- Tax-season organizer review against the firm's checklist.

## Current substitute behavior and cost

A bookkeeper, junior accountant, admin, or seasonal tax admin. Job evidence shows client account management, bank-transaction coding, reconciliations, payroll, and small-business record maintenance as paid work [S009, S010].

## Buying authority

In small practices the owner/CPA/bookkeeper controls spend — a fast yes. Larger firms add a partner/ops approval, which slows the close.

## Trust and risk

The main limiter. Financial records, PII, and professional liability make the first wedge **prep-only**: sort documents, identify missing items, draft follow-up, prepare checklists. Reconciliations, tax positions, filings, and client advice stay behind the owner's approval. Same confirmation-gate discipline as the contractor Employee.

## AI Employee upsell logic

Same buyer in small practices. The trigger: the owner likes the prep skill but still chases every client and moves every document. The Employee becomes the client-document coordinator and monthly-close assistant, drafting client messages behind approval.

## Reachability

Good and **desk-based** — Google Maps, state CPA directories, QuickBooks ProAdvisor / Xero advisor directories, local tax-prep searches, job posts. Phone reach is reliable in business hours; tax-season timing matters (off-peak is easier to set up).

## Expansion pattern

"Bookkeepers and similar" names the broader lane: **desk-based professional offices with recurring clients and document/admin labor** — tax prep, fractional CFO/CAS, payroll. After contractors and bookkeepers, these are the next pins (see [`../entrepreneurship-playbook.md`](../entrepreneurship-playbook.md), Moore's bowling-pin).
