# Segment — Freight Brokers / Staffing & Recruiting (Parked)

**Status: complete** · _Evaluated, parked. Our beachhead is paint/landscape contractors; see [`../00-decision.md`](../00-decision.md)._

Easiest segment to reach by phone, parked because the market pulls toward outbound volume (a lane AMTECH deliberately avoids) and competition is crowded.

## What's there

Freight brokerage and recruiting share a high-volume follow-up engine: calls, emails, sourcing, qualification, ATS/load-board organization. A broker reports ~4,000 calls in three months plus personalized follow-up [S021]; load coverage moves through email intake + phone confirmation [S022]; recruiters list sourcing, LinkedIn messages, ATS organization, and role prep/wrap-up [S023].

## Skill-shaped tasks (if revisited)

Prospect/candidate research briefs, follow-up queue cleaners, intake summarizers — **research/prep only, no autonomous outbound.**

## Substitute cost

Recruiting coordinator / sourcer / dispatcher: roughly $40–55k base [S024]; freight roles are often commission-heavy, which makes a direct-savings pitch harder.

## Why parked

Phone-native and easy to reach, but the buyer usually wants *more placements/loads*, not better admin hygiene; the space is crowded with ATS/CRM and AI recruiting tools [S024]; and outbound automation raises TCPA/deliverability/reputation risk. It pulls AMTECH toward lead-gen — explicitly out of scope. Parked unless run strictly as internal research/prep.
