# Segment — Marketing / SEO / Content Agencies (Not Our Buyer)

**Status: complete** · _Evaluated and rejected as a beachhead. Kept as a live "why not" so it isn't re-litigated. See [`../00-decision.md`](../00-decision.md) §2._

Marketing/SEO/content agencies look attractive — reachable, AI-comfortable, real reporting/fulfillment pain — but they are the **wrong buyer for AMTECH's product**, and this page records why so the question stays closed.

## Why agencies are wrong for us

1. **AI-native = can DIY = lowest value.** The worth of a done-for-you AI worker is *inversely* proportional to the buyer's own AI sophistication. An agency already has Claude/ChatGPT and often a technical person; shown an AI skill, the honest reaction is "I could build that myself." The buyers who need AMTECH are the ones who *can't* — AI-naive owner-operators.
2. **The skill-buyer and the Employee-buyer are opposite people.** Agencies might buy a cheap skill but will not pay $1,000+/mo for a worker they think they could approximate. Choosing agencies caps AMTECH at the low rung — the opposite of the recurring-Employee business.
3. **Maximum competition.** Agencies are the most AI-saturated, most cold-pitched inbox in the economy. A solo founder cold-calling is the 51st AI pitch that day.
4. **No owned asset / no local edge.** AMTECH owns the Estimate skill (contractor-fit), not an agency tool; and the founder's local, in-person advantage doesn't transfer to selling agencies nationally.

## The pain is real — it's just not ours to sell to

Agencies do feel fulfillment, reporting, and client-communication pain [S001–S003], and an account coordinator is a real paid substitute [S004]. But pain ≠ buyability for *this* product and *this* founder. The same recurring-back-office thesis is far stronger aimed at AI-naive contractors who own the decision and can't self-serve.

## If ever revisited

Only as a **channel** (an agency reselling AMTECH Employees to its SMB clients), and only after the contractor beachhead is won — never as a direct beachhead. Until then: closed.
