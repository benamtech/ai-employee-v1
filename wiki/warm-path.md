# Warm-Path Inventory

**Status: complete** · _Revised 2026-06-27 for the contractor (paint/landscape) beachhead._

## First rule

Before working the cold list, ask warm contacts for **painter/landscaper intros**. One warm contractor intro is worth more than 40 cold rows this week — and Ben is local, so a face-to-face demo is on the table.

## Friend / network referral prompt

"Quick favor — do you know any painters or landscapers running their own crew? I built something that writes their estimates for them, in their own pricing and format, off the photos and messages from a job. I just want to show one of them on a real job they pick — no charge to look."

## Past-client / contact prompt

"I'm testing a new AMTECH tool with contractors: it writes their estimates for them — their pricing, their format, within about 5% — and they approve it before it goes out. If you know a painter, landscaper, or hardscaper drowning in estimates at night, would you connect us? I'll run their next real estimate free."

## Testimonial request (after delivery)

"Was it close enough to what you'd have charged that I can quote you? One sentence: how accurate it was, how long an estimate normally takes you, and whether you'd tell another contractor to look at it."

## First 10 conversations

1. Any painter / landscaper / hardscaper Ben already knows personally.
2. Friends or family who know a contractor running their own crew.
3. Local Scranton/PA trade contacts — supply-house relationships, trade groups.
4. Any contractor Ben has ever done website or other work for.
5. Anyone who recently hired a painter/landscaper and can intro the owner.

Ask every person for **one name**, not a sale.

## Secondary warm path (beachhead #2 — later)

When the bookkeeper doc-sorter skill exists, run the same play with any accountant/bookkeeper in Ben's network: "It sorts the client-document pile and drafts the missing-items chase — no accounting judgment, just kills the sorting."
